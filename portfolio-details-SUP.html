Project Overview

For the course "Client Projects" we were tasked to make a game for a client. At the start of the semester we had the chance to choose which client we wanted to work for (out of a list of clients). After this we were paired into groups with other students who chose the same client. We chose for the Belgian Navy/Airforce.

The first weeks was mostly brainstorming about possible ideas. We already knew that the client did not want a "War Game". We each researched an idea. I focused on an army engineering concept, which is the one we ultimately chose. We also visited the navy in Zeebrugge and the airbase in Kokzijde for research.

The game takes place on a Belgian frigate in the middle of the ocean. You play as an Army engineer who has to repair and maintain drones and helicopters. By replacing parts, power washing and refueling. At the start, the player gets a certain amount of tasks. The game is finished when all of these tasks are completed. However, it is also possible to break things and cause hazards!

Client Projects was an elective course for me, so most of my group members were studying a different major (Independent Game Production). Therefore, we worked with Unity, as it is the program they are most familiar with.

Features (I worked on)

Taking / Placing / Snapping Objects

The player can place / replace parts on the vehicle.

Screwing

The player can unscrew and screw in plates on the vehicle.

Chaos Elements

The player can cause fires and electrical blasts

Game Manager

The entire game is controlled by the game manager. A system that enables a certain amount of tasks at random.

Task sequence

Some tasks can only be performed after other tasks are already completed.
